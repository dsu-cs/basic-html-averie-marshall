Behavior

Lynx are solitary animals. They rely on stealth and surprise to catch their prey. They are mostly nocturnal. They can also climb trees! The only time they seem to consistently socialize is for mating season which is in late winter.

Lynx kittens are highly playful and dependent on their mother for almost a year. They partake in social play such as wrestling and chasing each other. When they are just 2-3 months old they begin to learn to hunt by watching their mother. They are born in litters of 1-5, and sometimes even up to 6-8 when food is abundant!

Prey

The Canada Lynx has a variety of prey from small ground animals, quick racing hares, and even some birds.

Prey Type Description
Snowshoe Hare Snowshoe Hares are the primary prey of lynx. Around 60-90% of the lynx diet completely relies on these hares. Their fur changes with the seasons as their main source of camoflauge
Red Squirrels Red Squirrels range in color despite their name. They can be any shade from vivid ginger to dark brown. These animals are a crucial secondary food source for lynx
Grouse Grouse are a type of bird that live in the same northern habitats as lynx. They are another important food source for the lynx, especially when snowshoe hare populations are low
